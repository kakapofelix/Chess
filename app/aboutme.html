Awarded Titles

  • Grandmaster 2007
  • Member of the Olympic Team
  • U18 World Champion

    • I was introduced to chess back in 1995, at age 8. My father taught me the basics, I used to go to the local chess club with him. I got my FIDE rating, 2135 in 1997. My first victory in an international tournament was in 1999. 2001 was the tourning point in my carrier. I managed to win both the rapid and the traditional U14 hungarian championship with 9/9 thus qualifying the world youth championship. There I also won, with 9 out of 11 including victories against Nakamura and Tomashevsky. That was the time I became private student at my local high school, and started to concentrate on chess mainly. Shortly after finishing school I scored my last GM norms in 2007 and reached 2575, and from then on I consider myself as professional.

    I am member of the Hungarian National Team since 2011. Best performance there: 4,5/7 on 2nd board, 2765 in 2017, Crete.
    Most significant result: in Minsk 2017, I qualified to the World Cup, there I managed to beat B. Amin in the first round, then lost to P. Svidler in rapid tie-breaks.
    My most important results as a Junior: World team champion U16, 2003, TUR, Denizli European team champion U18, 2003, HUN, Balatonlelle

    Trainers I worked with: IM Ferenc Berebora 2001-2002, GM Peter Lukacs 2003-2011
